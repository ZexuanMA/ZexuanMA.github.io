► Deep learning-based GPR inversion

Ground penetrating radar (GPR) techniques are widely used in planetary exploration, archaeological prospecting, geological hazard assessment, etc. We developed a GPR inversion operator to support real-time and reliable imaging of near-surface permittivity and resistivity structures simultaneously.

Xue et al., 2024b

► Earthquake monitoring and prediction

Seismic electric signals (SES) are essential short-term precursors of earthquakes. We developed a Real-time Automatic Search Engine (RASE) for SES detection, thus realizing the potential of geoelectric field observation in earthquake monitoring and disaster prevention.

Xue et al., 2022, 2023

The ultralow-frequency (ULF) EM anomaly is also considered one of the earthquake precursory signals. We employed a multireference station data-quality-weighted method to detect the ULF anomaly. Statistical tests indicate that the ULF anomalies appearing approximately two months before the earthquake are likely driven by physical processes.

Xue et al., 2024a
